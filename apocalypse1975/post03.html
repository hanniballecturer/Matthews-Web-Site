Part 3 On Joining a Cult

Could you join a cult?

Are you the sort of person that might want something better out of life? Are you the sort of person who would like more money? Would you like to see the world changed for the better? Would you like to feel more spiritual and closer to the divine? Perhaps you want to be as far away from religion as possible? Would you just for once like to be actually really good at something? Is there one idea or belief that you wished all others shared?

Is there something you desire?

Did you answered yes to any of the above questions?

Well, there is likely a cult with your name on it.

I feel shame having been in a cult. I also feel confusion too. I was raised in the Christadelphians but it wasn't until I became involved in the International Church of Christ that I really grasped how monstrous cults are.

It has taken over a decade to figure out that something deeply damaging had happened to me. It has taken more than a decade to open up and start talking about the abuse. For much of that time I have blamed myself.

I find there is a problem though! How do I explain what happened to me without sounding like a complete nutter? Was I really so weak and pathetic to be drawn into something so damaging.

Was I really so stupid?

So, here goes, let's try and explain how the machine works. This was my experience, perhaps some of this will make sense.
First things first, nobody ever joins a cult!

When you join a cult it never has a sign on the door saying “we are a cult”. If you really knew it was a cult you simply wouldn’t bother. Long after I joined and ultimately left I still wasn't clear that I had been in a cult.

The shop window of a cult always misrepresents what is actually on sale. Don't bother asking the members, they cannot be honest about how they think and feel. They will suppress their true doubts and feelings. They neglect to mention their personal difficulties whilst greeting you with joy and charm. The inner workings of the cult e.g. finance will not be open to scrutiny nor will the revolving door of members joining and leaving. Frequently the group history is rewritten and adjusted to cover up past scandals and failure. "Yes, mistakes were made" and "things have changed" will be the mantras to outsiders. Dirty secrets remain stacked in every dark corner and never discussed.

What happens when you join a cult might be something like the following.

You have a leaflet/friend/family member who has a fantastic opportunity for faith/wealth/political change who would like to invite you to a meeting. When you attend the meeting everybody seems really friendly and knowledgeable. The leader is slick and charismatic; many of his points resonate with you. Some things seem a bit odd, but hey – you don’t need to agree with everything. Certainly what they are offering seems great and there are so many people with success stories. You agree to meet “one more time” and also go out on a social activity with your friend and a few of his fellow members. Everybody is really interested in you and they seem to be able to provide exactly the answer you are seeking.

To capture you, the cult must connect with your desires.

Do you desire more money? Perhaps the cult will offer a system for making extra cash in your spare time.

Want to feel closer to God? A cult might catch your attention by their apparently reformed lives and impressive Bible knowledge.

Feel a need to create political change? You might be impressed with the turnout to their rallies and meetings.

Desires always beg the question how much is enough? How much is enough money, spirituality or political change? What will be sufficient to satisfy? Your desires become a carrot on a stick. A stick you will soon discover of ever increasing length.

You may feel some sense of shame too. You have wasted all of this time chasing after your heart's desire and these people seem to have nailed it. What have you been messing about at?

The cult makes a proposal, “we have the answer”.

“We have the answer and we have the system to satisfy your desire. After all, look at the people around you who are already members. These were people just like you who have changed their lives by following our system, you can do this too."

"Think positively, have a little faith!”

The cult makes itself a monument to what committed people can achieve. This monument casts a shadow on your own efforts. Honestly, are you really committed? Are you really doing your best? Your unfulfilled desires become a weapon, a rod to beat you with shame and humiliation. After all, "if you were totally committed you would not need our help!"

The offer is made more credible by coming from people you trust. It might be your brother, a friend or your partner. Perhaps you simply trust the leader of the group, they seem so genuine. Perhaps the message also chimes with some existing faulty belief that you didn't know you had.

You take that first small step of trust.

Then you encounter the first bait and switch.

A door just closed behind you and you didn't even notice.

Now they have you!

Want to make more money? You need to start buying our products.

Want to feel closer to God? You need to follow your Bible study leader.

Want to change the world? You need to read more of the literature so that you know how to argue your case.

What just happened is that your legitimate desire has now been substituted for the needs of the group.

You will buy this tactic because you want your own needs met. You will buy this tactic because others lay claim to success. You will buy this tactic because you feel small and ashamed. You will buy this tactic because you feel excited about the prospect of change.

You follow the advice regardless of common sense or instinct. There may be an initial hit of excitement, it feels good but soon it fades. You are left with a nagging sense that you are still not having your desires met. "Is this working?" You may feel in your heart that satisfaction is slipping away from you but you want another shot of the good stuff, perhaps that is the key?

Then comes the next bait and switch.

Another door closes silently behind you.

“Yes I know how you feel at this stage, I felt the same and wondered if it was all worth it but I found there were still other things I needed to do.”

Is there still no money coming in? Start inviting your friends and family to sales meetings.

Do you still feel no closer to God? Make sure you attend all of the meetings, "it really helped me".

Is the world not changing? You need to get out on the street and spread the message.

Just try a bit harder, it will be worth it.

You feel another hit. You are now on a slippery slope of following the cult's advice whilst waiting for lasting satisfaction. Your desperation becomes more profound as fulfillment becomes more elusive.

Lies dressed as advice are never revealed all at once, you are initiated into the smaller lies first then graduating to the bigger ones. You don't even need to believe the lies with any true conviction. Ensure you provide the right answer when asked on questions of doctrine, shout and clap at the right moment in meetings, do as you are told, ignore your doubts and questions.

Fake it till you make it becomes your motto, but you will call it faith.

Some advising you may genuinely believe the advice or at least bits of it. You follow where they lead because you trust them. Your dissatisfaction with yourself is now even more profound.

This now sends one very clear message, the problem is you!

Bait and switches do not form an orderly queue. They start as a gentle rain and then form a torrent. This advice is supposed to satisfy your legitimate desire but instead it drags you deeper into the system.

In parallel other things are happening too.

You are now part of a culture of like minded individuals who are all sinking deeper into the ideological quick sand of the group. The common experience starts to bind you together in your shared pretense, determination, desperation and frustration. Some really deep friendships start to form.

A second strand starts to appear too. As one bait and switch is layered on the other you realise that more and more time is being spent immersing yourself in the group. Evenings and weekends are taken up with meetings; money seems to fly out of your account as you “invest” in campaigning, travelling or tithing.

You are though a person of limited resources, there are only so many hours in the week, so many pay cheques a month, and you only have so much tolerance for the growing mental and emotional exhaustion. With no time to think clearly your life is now being entirely assimilated by the cult. Your previous friends and family seem remote. Perhaps you have been advised to move on from the "dead wood" that won’t convert and concentrate on pastures new? Perhaps your friends and family have also stepped back too, perhaps they are tired of you going on at them all the time?

You start to think more fully as a cult member. Your primary concerns become preserving the image of the group, increasing the membership and maintaining the flow of cash. Perhaps from time to time you gasp for breath and remember the desire you once had. It seems a remote and forgotten dream. All that matters now is supporting the cultic system and your fellow members.

Leaving will now be hugely traumatic, you have invested too much, you would be committing an act of betrayal. Consider the cost if you have family members in the cult or you have married into the cult? What if you now have children who are members? You are painfully aware that those who leave the cult are held up as failures, apostates, objects of ridicule and isolation. You soon realise that leaving will cost you every significant relationship you have left. More than that, any condemnation of the system must equate to condemnation of yourself, you now have blood on your hands, you are complicit!

Chasing the cult's way of salvation is addictive, you cannot stop. You feel terrified. If you let go there is nothing for you on the outside, only oblivion and isolation.

The cult knows this.

If you fail to meet the targets, sell the products, invite new members and do as you are told, the cult will threaten dismissal to force compliance. The cult may also apply mental, emotional or sexual violence to ensure this.

Resist or fail to comply and you will be chewed up, spat out and lose everything.
