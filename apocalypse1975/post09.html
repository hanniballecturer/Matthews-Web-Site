Bang!

I awoke in a stranger’s bed. She lay beside me asleep. My head was pounding and my mouth was bone dry. An ash tray had been knocked over onto the sheets; they stank of alcohol sweat and cigarettes. As my eyes started to focus I found myself surrounded by cuddly toys. The walls were covered with posters of Daniel O'Donnell. It is funny the weird details that stay in your recollections, especially at a time of crisis. In my head four words were on permanent loop “I don’t care anymore!”

I had just exploded a stack of dynamite under my life!

There was nothing heroic here, nothing noble, there was no great plan. Desperate measures were all I had left. I felt like I had been fired from a canon, thrown clear by a blast. The only problem with this sort of escape is that it both the blast and the subsequent impact on landing are unlikely to leave you in a good state of health. It was inevitably going to leave plenty of other casualties too.

Within days I opened the Leicester Mercury and started to browse through the adverts for accommodation. I rang one number, no reply. I rang a second number for a flat “off the Narborough Road”, they picked up and we arranged a viewing the following day. I wasn’t quite clear though what “off the Narborough Road” actually meant, so I decided to drive over and take a look. The flat was above a chip shop in a really rough area, it was clearly a shit hole miles from town. Worse still, it was an expensive shit hole. I picked up the phone to cancel the appointment and accidently rang the first number I tried. This time they picked up. “Could I come in that afternoon?”

It was a block of flats in the city centre; about five minutes walk from work. We looked at a few options that were pretty basic. The lady guiding me around said there was one last option available. It was a beautiful fully furnished one bedroom top floor apartment. There was a mezzanine area and a spiral staircase. I always wanted a spiral staircase. It was expensive but in theory within budget.

I started to do the sums. Shit there was a massive shortfall, how could I afford to do this? The blood drained from my face, then I realised I had put the rent on the spreadsheet twice.

There was a problem though; it wouldn’t be available for me to move in for four week. I was now technically homeless.

There were friends around me, not part of the cult. They played such an important role at this time. For the next four weeks I sofa surfed and slept in spare rooms so as not to annoy any one person for too long. I feel a huge debt of gratitude for their kindness.

There was no lift in the place so a friend and I carried what possessions I had up the many flights of stairs. There was a bookshelf, a computer and I seem to remember a wooden spoon. Most of my life consisted of a couple of guitars, CDs, DVD and a few books.

Material possessions suddenly seemed rather unimportant. I had a roof over my head, a bed to sleep in and most of all I was free.

But what does freedom mean to a person who has had their freedoms systematically stripped away?

When I called it a day on the Christadelphians as a teenager I refused to attend the meetings and did my own thing. On a practical level I was free but inside I was anything but free. Their ideas and beliefs had taken root in my mind like bind weed. I lacked the social skills and appropriate world views to cope with life on the outside. When life became hard as I grieved the death of my father I had little to fall back on other than magical thinking and religious guilt. What I was going through was surely my own fault as I was the one who had betrayed both God and my parents.

I was now trying to leave this unhealthy world of spiritual and cultic abuse for a second time. At one point I was reading the suicide notes of Virginia Wolfe to cheer myself up, this was not a good sign.

I really thought this was it. My life was over.

I sat in a nearby pub one lunch time and absolutely couldn’t imagine what my life might be like from this point on. I had my job and that felt pretty much like it.

I was now free and could do whatever I liked but with too many options I was stuck.

I started going out at night, something I didn’t do in my other life. I watched people and felt like an outsider. I was fascinated by the queues outside of different night clubs, did I have the courage to go in and see what it was like? One night I did. I remember sitting in Lloyd’s Number One Bar under the stairs feeling so small and alien. For context who those who may not know Lloyd’s, it is not exactly the Ritz. I looked at the people dancing; they all seemed so cool and together. Over the years I use this experience as a bench mark of how far I have come. From time to time I sit in the same place looking at the crowds and think “God this place is terrible”.

My friends were all telling me that I should now have some fun. Enjoy life a little, get out and date a bit, nothing too serious. The problem with real freedom is that it is terrifying. So what did I do? I became involved in a pretty serious relationship. But that is story outside of this one.

I made a mistake, but the point is that it was my mistake. If freedom means anything then it must mean that we are allowed to make our own mistakes. This was not some mistake foisted upon me by an outside force it was 100% my own cock up.

Making so many changes in my life placed me into survival mode. There were so many practical matters to sort out both good and bad. Many things other people take for granted, insurance, and banking arrangements. Given I didn’t own a car I had to figure out ways of getting about the country. One Christmas I drove to the family Christmas party with a hire car. I remember passing the speed camera and seeing the flash in my rear view mirror. There was a run of other issues that came in at the same time. I distinctly recall thinking that God was punishing me.

I didn’t care anymore. The fear of Hell fire had kept me in the cult well beyond my sell-by date. I had finally concluded that going to Hell was infinitely more preferable than spending another moment in that shitty group.

Freedom was not simply freedom to make my own mistakes it required something deeper. I had to set about clearing out all of the crap in my head that shitty religion had placed there.

There was practical crap, emotional crap, mental crap and also relational crap.

Relationships were a concern. There was period of people from the ICC still trying to be in touch. In some cases this was fine in other cases I viewed the efforts with suspicion. Certain friends and family made a genuine effort to stay in touch. I believe that they were showing genuine concern. Other people seemed far more interested in finding a salve for their own conscience. A few people in leadership wanted to meet to meet to “apologise”. “To what end?” I asked myself. I declined the offer deciding that keeping a wide birth was a much safer bet. They can stick their apologies up their arses. There were also plenty of people who dropped me like a stone. Many years later I want to Birmingham for the first time. I was still deeply apprehensive about bumping into somebody from the cult.

Just prior to my sofa surfing, when I was reading the suicide letters of Virginia Wolfe I reached out to a friend from church. In fairness he drove over from Birmingham that night to see me. He kept telling me that it was down to me to decide what I wanted to do. Clearly I chose to leave and call it a day but I couldn’t help but feel that he was glad to see the back of me.

As with any breakup there is also the inevitable choosing of sides that takes place among long standing friends. The ones that hurt the most were those outside of the group. They picked a side based on what limited information they had to hand. Given the manner of my departure and the fact that I wasn’t either able or willing to provide further context to the long and painful history leading up to the event it was hardly surprising.

A couple of years ago I took on an allotment. It has proved to be powerful tool for healing and also a metaphor for cult recovery.

I had received an email from the Council telling me that my application was successful. I replied stating that I was willing to take the plot on. The lady at the Council replied “have you seen the plot?” “No” I responded. This sounded ominous. I popped over to take a look, the plot was 11b. I found plot 11. Next door was six foot of weeds. I kept walking, plot 12! There was a couple working on plot 11. I asked if they knew which section was plot 11b. He shrugged and looked annoyed. “There”, directing me with a nod of his head pointing at the six foot of weeds. There was something about the plot that I could relate to. It had suffered neglect for many years and just needed some time and some TLC. Not afraid of a bit of hard work I took the plot on. There have been many times when digging the soil dragging out weeds and seeing the crops start to develop how similar my own healing process has been.

So I bagged the lot, God, church and the Bible. To some extent I bagged the morality that had been sold to me from the back of a lorry. I chucked it all in the bin, my faith and relationships in the church. All abandoned, the hymns the prayers, sod it all. I was angry and I was done with this shit.

I wanted to party, I wanted to explore other relationships I wanted to see the world. I made more choices, some good some bad. I travelled to Thailand and Australia.

One day a friend of mine asked for my help.

Her husband had died and there was a problem in the garden shed. He kept a fridge in the shed full of meat. The problem was that the fridge was faulty; it had been there for “some time now”.

Not really understanding what I was getting into I offered to help clear it out.

In some sections the meat was still frozen solid, that was the easy bit. In other sections the meat had become rotten and putrid. It stank to high heaven. I put on rubber gloves and started to wretch as I shoveled out the festering mess.

I had my own store of rotting badness to contend with. Slapping a lid on what had happened to me was not resolving anything; I had to process it all. I had to make sense of the pain, betrayal and misinformation. Yes, latterly the allotment has formed a more encouraging metaphor but I had to start at the core of the problem. I had to cover my face and start to dig out all of the festering shit I was trying not to think about.

So I started to dig.
